Why you can trust it with money

Bold where it's safe.
Deferential where it counts.

The model handles the ambiguity — reading messy text, diagnosing the real cause. The money decision is a deterministic, bounded computation that no prompt or model output can bypass.

Constrained autonomy

Acts on simulated money without a human in the common case — but only inside hard, non-overridable limits.

shield

Deterministic guardrails

Coupon caps, refund ≤ value, auto-ceiling, rate limits — evaluated in code, never bypassable by request or model output.

The decision pipeline

One graph. Six steps. Two branch points.

Every request flows through the same auditable path — and the money decision is a defensible constrained optimisation, not opaque model output.

01

Triage

Classify the issue and extract ids — treating the customer's text strictly as untrusted data.

02

Context + Policy

Fetch the order & customer via MCP, retrieve the governing policy with citations.

03

Risk

Score fraud/abuse from real signals — bounded, explainable, model can't suppress it.

04

Plan

Pick the minimum-cost eligible remedy under the satisfaction floor (§9.4 selection).

05

Guardrails

Validate against hard caps — clamp, pass, or force a human.

06

Act or escalate

Execute the audited action idempotently, or pause for review. Reply, log, emit events.
